Women's Fashion


Women's fashion in the 1910s featured softer, more natural silhouettes. Wider brims on hats and short hair became popular as well.


Some examples of women's fashion throughout the decade


Orientalism also began to rise at the beginning of the decade, set off by a ballet called Schéhérazade performed by the Ballets Russes in Paris in 1910.


An example of orientalism-styled clothing


Men's Fashion


Men's fashion in the 1910s often involed suits, jackets, and cuffed ankle length pants..


Examples of men's fashion throughout the decade


World War I


World War I taking place slowed the production and distribution of new fashions. After the war ended, the simple styles of the 1910s continued while introducing more cylindrical silhouettes. These new silhouettes would develop into a new style in the next decade.
