There was many historical events that took place in the United States during the 1910s. This page will list some of the interesting or important events that took place each year.


U.S. Timeline (1910-1919)


1910

  • The Boy Scouts of America is founded
  • The Great Fire of 1910 takes place
  • The Mann-Elkinks Act is passed
  • The Mann Act is passed

1911

  • The Triangle Shirtwaist Factory fire takes place
  • The first Indianapolis 500 is staged
  • The Mona Lisa is stolen

1912

  • The Titanic sinks
  • New Mexico and Arizona become states
  • The first Indianapolis 500 is staged
  • The Girl Scouts of America is founded
  • Woodrow Wilson is elected president

  • Woman suffrage parade of 1913 takes place

  • The Adamson Railway Labora Act is passed

  • The Jones Act is passed

1917

  • United States enters World War I
  • The Espionage Act of 1917 is passed
  • The Lansing-Ishii Agreement is signed
  • The National Hockey League is formed
  • The U.S. Virgin Islands are purchased from Denmark

1918

  • The Fourteen Points are described by President Wilson
  • The U.S. uses aircrafts in war for the first time.
  • A deadly Influenza epidemic begins at Fort Riley, Kansas

1919

  • President Wilson suffers a massive stroke
  • U.S. Senate rejects Treaty of Versailles and League of Nations
  • The 18th Amendment is ratified
  • The 19th Amendment is ratified
